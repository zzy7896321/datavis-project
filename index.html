FDIC Failed Banks From Oct 2000 to Dec 2016

During Oct 2000 through Dec 2016, over 500 FDIC-insured banks failed in the US, a lot of which happened during the 2008 financial crisis. Thanks to FDIC, no insured funds have ever been lost despite the huge volume of failed banks. The following chart shows the yearly aggregated statistics of the failed banks.

The FDIC failed bank data were collected from FDIC Failures and Assistance Transactions - Historical Statistics on Banking and Failed Bank List. The US map is derived from the course material and city coordinates were crawled from www.latlong.net.

Top Acquirers

Though the most quantity of failed banks were not acquired by any institution and thus were covered by the FDIC during 2000-2016, the FDIC is not the largest source of funds to resolve the issue. OneWest Bank, FSB acquired the most banks in terms of estimated loss, while JP Morgan Chase Bank acquired the most banks in terms of total assets or deposits.
